Deneread

Extract extraction system for articles and books

How?

Deneread software extracts related source document paragraphs

Usable in web upload or computer application, get additional context information on the material you're reading or writing
The main points of the input are highlighted for you, and those are used to fetch you additional information

Examples: Books

The following line is highlighted by deneread as the main concept of the larger text that was parsed.
The threat of revolution and social unrest causes the elite to limit economic redistribution and democratization.
Books can be specified, or deneread can find them via google search. Results from the books are then returned (below).
Why nations fail (page 5)
A country starts as a nondemocratic society in which a small rich group controls most of the wealth and rules the poor majority. As the ruling class, the rich receive taxation from the economy's output and they decide on the taxation rate as the only means of extraction.
Captial in the Twenty-First Century (page 690)
The world today is returning towards "patrimonial capitalism", in which much of the economy is dominated by inherited wealth: the power of this economic class is increasing, threatening to create an oligarchy.

Examples: Articles

Now suppose you want to look at articles instead of books for the same extracted highlight.
The threat of revolution and social unrest causes the elite to limit economic redistribution and democratization.
You can limit yourself to research articles, or all articles on the web, such as news articles. Results from the articles are returned (below).
SOCIAL CONFLICT AND REDISTRIBUTIVE PREFERENCES AMONG RICH AND POOR (Conclusions, page 60)
Social unrest puts pressure on high-income earners to reconcile such conflict. Thus, the argument in this paper is that high-income earners consider inequality to be too large when the conflict increases to a level that threatens their property and safety.
Globalism and Inequality Are the Real Threats to Our Democracy (Chapter: Threat to Democracy, page 82)
But when a society is deeply divided along lines of wealth and income, there is a concern that one social group might capture all the branches of government that are supposed to check each other, and that this group will use government to oppress the other social groups.
